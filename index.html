Thai Workforce Informality by Province, 2024

The Thai Ministry of Labor defines informal workers as those operating within the "informal economy" without employee status under the Labor Protection Act. In most cases, Thai so- cial security laws extend only to formal workers, and the limited legal protections available to informal workers are rarely enforced. Consequently, informal workers are deprived of essential social security benefits, including workers' compensation, unemployment and retirement insurance, and coverage for work-related accidents, injuries and illnesses, as well as access to information regarding their legal rights.

Kongtip et al. "Informal Workers in Thailand: Occupational Health and Social Security Disparities." New Solutions, 2015.
